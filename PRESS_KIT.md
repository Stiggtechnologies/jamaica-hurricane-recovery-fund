## Sample Press Release

### FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

**Jamaica Hurricane Recovery Fund Launches $100 Million Campaign to Rebuild Communities and Strengthen Climate Resilience**

*Jamaican-born entrepreneur Orville Davis leads global initiative to support hurricane recovery across all 14 parishes*

**[CITY, DATE]** – The Jamaica Hurricane Recovery Fund (JHRF) today announced the official launch of its ambitious $100 million campaign to support comprehensive hurricane relief, recovery, and long-term climate resilience across Jamaica.

Founded by Orville Davis, a Jamaican-born entrepreneur based in Alberta, Canada, JHRF addresses the urgent need for sustainable rebuilding following devastating hurricane damage to homes, schools, and infrastructure across the island nation.

"Jamaica gave me my foundation, and now it's time to help rebuild foundations for others," said Davis. "This fund represents a commitment from the global Jamaican diaspora and international partners to ensure our communities don't just recover—they thrive with resilience against future storms."

The campaign focuses on four critical areas:
- Reconstructing damaged homes with climate-resilient designs
- Restoring schools and educational facilities
- Building emergency shelters and community infrastructure
- Implementing long-term climate preparedness programs

JHRF operates with complete financial transparency, publishing quarterly reports and maintaining community oversight committees. The organization has partnered with Stigg Security Inc., Omega Group, and Alberta Tech Team to maximize operational efficiency and impact.

"This initiative brings together the Jamaican diaspora, corporate partners, and local communities in an unprecedented collaboration," Davis added. "Every donation, no matter the size, brings hope to families and communities working to rebuild their lives."

The fund welcomes donations from individuals, corporations, and organizations worldwide. For more information or to contribute, visit jamaicahurricanerecoveryfund.org.

### About Jamaica Hurricane Recovery Fund
The Jamaica Hurricane Recovery Fund is a humanitarian organization dedicated to supporting Jamaica's recovery from hurricane damage while building long-term climate resilience. Founded in 2024, JHRF operates with complete transparency and community oversight across all 14 parishes of Jamaica.

## Interview Questions & Answers

### For Orville Davis

**Q: What inspired you to start the Jamaica Hurricane Recovery Fund?**
A: Growing up in Jamaica, I witnessed the devastating impact hurricanes have on our communities. After building my career in Canada, I felt a responsibility to give back and create something that would have lasting impact—not just immediate relief, but true resilience.

**Q: How is JHRF different from other disaster relief organizations?**
A: We focus on long-term solutions, not just immediate aid. We're rebuilding with climate-resilient designs, empowering communities to lead their own recovery, and maintaining 100% financial transparency. Plus, we're mobilizing the global Jamaican diaspora in unprecedented ways.

**Q: What does the $100 million goal represent?**
A: It represents comprehensive recovery: thousands of homes rebuilt, dozens of schools restored, emergency shelters constructed, and communities equipped with the knowledge and infrastructure to withstand future storms. It's about building Jamaica's future, not just repairing the past.

**Q: How can people contribute if they can't donate financially?**
A: There are many ways to help: volunteer your professional skills, spread awareness on social media, organize fundraisers in your community, or connect us with potential corporate partners. Visit our Get Involved page for specific opportunities.

**Q: What's your vision for Jamaica's future?**
A: I see a Jamaica where communities are resilient, where every family has a safe home, where children can attend school without interruption, and where people have the resources and knowledge to face climate challenges with confidence.
